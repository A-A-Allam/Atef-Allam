Slide 3: What is Neointimal Hyperplasia?

Definition:

Aberrant thickening of the intima post-vascular injury.

Key Triggers:

  • Angioplasty/stent deployment.
  • Surgical trauma (CABG, AV fistulas).

Visual:

Cross-section of artery (normal vs. NIH).

Slide 4: Clinical Impact of NIH

  • In-Stent Restenosis (ISR): 20–30% with BMS, <10% with DES.
  • Vein Graft Failure: 40–50% occlusion at 10 years.
  • AV Fistula Dysfunction: 50% failure within 1 year.

Graph:

Restenosis rates across interventions (bar chart).

Slide 5: Pathophysiology of NIH (Flowchart)

Endothelial Injury → Platelet adhesion → PDGF/TGF-β release.

Inflammation: Macrophages (IL-6, TNF-α), ROS.

SMC Proliferation: Phenotypic switch → ECM deposition.

Remodeling: Fibrosis → lumen narrowing.

Visual:

Animated flowchart with icons.

Slide 6: Key Molecular Pathways

  • PDGF/PDGFR: SMC migration.
  • mTOR/PI3K-Akt: Targeted by sirolimus.
  • TGF-β/Smad: Fibrosis.

Graphic:

Pathway diagram with drug targets highlighted.

Slide 7: Mechanical Strategies to Prevent NIH

Drug-Eluting Stents (DES):

  • Sirolimus/Paclitaxel: ↓ SMC proliferation.
  • ESC/EACTS Class I recommendation.

Drug-Coated Balloons (DCB):

Paclitaxel for ISR.

Bioresorbable Scaffolds:

Temporary support.

Image:

DES vs. BMS efficacy comparison.

Slide 8: Pharmacological Therapies

  • DAPT: Aspirin + P2Y12 inhibitors (6–12 months).
  • Statins: Plaque stabilization (high-intensity atorvastatin).
  • Colchicine: COLCOT trial (↓23% ischemia).
  • Novel Agents: SGLT2 inhibitors, IL-1β antagonists.

Slide 9: Local and Gene-Based Therapies

  • Perivascular Wraps: Paclitaxel-eluting meshes (CABG).
  • siRNA Nanoparticles: Inclisiran (PCSK9 inhibition).
  • EPC Capture Stents: Promote endothelialization.
