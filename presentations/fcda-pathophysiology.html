Fibrocystic Disease of Arteries (FCDA): Pathophysiology & Management

Medical Presentation Professor Atef Allam

Fibrocystic Arterial Disease: From Medial Degeneration to Aneurysmal Rupture

Slide 2: Key Learning Objectives

  • Define FCDA and its histopathological hallmarks
  • Identify predilection sites (renal, aortic, carotid)
  • Explain aneurysm formation mechanisms
  • Review guideline-based management
  • Explore future directions (biologics, gene therapy)

Slide 3: What is FCDA?

Definition:

  • Non-atherosclerotic, non-inflammatory arteriopathy
  • Triad: Cystic degeneration + fibrosis + elastic fiber fragmentation

Visual:

Histology image (H&E stain: mucoid cysts in media)

Key Quote:

"A disease of the arterial media, not the intima"

Slide 4: Arterial Predilection (Hotspots)

Ranked Involvement:

  • Renal arteries (30-50%): HTN, aneurysms
  • Thoracic aorta (20%): Dissection risk
  • Carotids/vertebrals (15%): Stroke/TIA
  • Mesenteric/iliac (10%): Ischemic colitis

Visual:

Anatomical map with color-coded risk zones

Slide 5: Why Aneurysms Form in FCDA

Pathophysiological Cascade:

MMP overexpression → ECM degradation

Cystic medial necrosis → focal wall weakness

Hypertension → radial stress → dilation

Visual:

Animated artery wall breakdown

Slide 6: Aneurysm Risk Stratification

Artery High-Risk Size Intervention Threshold
Renal >1.5 cm Endovascular repair
Thoracic aorta >3.5 cm Open surgery
Carotid >2 cm Stenting

Visual:

Size comparison graphic (aneurysm vs. normal)

Slide 7: Diagnostic Workflow

Step 1:

CTA/MRA (luminal irregularities + cysts)

Step 2:

Confirm with histology (biopsy/resection)

Step 3:

Rule out mimics (FMD, vasculitis)

Visual:

Diagnostic algorithm flowchart

Slide 8: Management Strategies

Medical Therapy:

  • ACE inhibitors (TGF-β modulation)
  • Statins (pleiotropic effects)

Surgical:

  • EVAR for aortic aneurysms
  • Drug-eluting stents for renal stenosis

Visual:

Treatment infographic

Slide 9: Emerging Therapies

  • MMP inhibitors (doxycycline trials)
  • TGF-β monoclonal antibodies
  • Stem cell-seeded grafts

Visual:

Molecular targets diagram

Slide 10: Case Example

Patient:

52M with resistant HTN

Findings:

  • Renal artery "string of beads" on CTA
  • Aneurysm (1.8 cm)

Treatment:

  • Stent placement + ramipril

Visual:

Pre/post stent images

Slide 11: Guidelines Snapshot

ESC 2023:

  • Screen renal arteries in young-onset HTN
  • Operate at smaller aneurysm sizes vs. atherosclerosis

AHA 2022:

  • Lifelong imaging surveillance

Slide 12: Future Directions

  • Genetic profiling (familial cases)
  • Nanoparticle drug delivery
  • AI-based rupture prediction

Visual:

Concept art of smart stent

Slide 13: Summary & Take-Home Points

  • FCDA targets renal/aortic/carotid beds
  • Aneurysms rupture earlier - monitor aggressively
  • Multimodal therapy (meds + endo/surgery)
